The domain of staged performance: actors follow scripts on a stage before
an audience, producing an illusion that is internally coherent but
fundamentally constructed. As a source domain, theatre foregrounds the
gap between appearance and reality -- the performance looks convincing
precisely because it is designed to. The key structural feature is
performance-as-deception: the audience knows (or should know) that what
they see is scripted spectacle, not spontaneous reality. When this
awareness fails -- when the audience mistakes the play for the world --
the theatrical metaphor becomes a tool for diagnosing intellectual error,
as in Bacon's Idols of the Theatre.
