The domain of paid work and employment -- jobs, tasks, shifts, queues,
supervision, hiring, and firing. Distinct from economics proper (which
concerns exchange and markets) in that labor focuses on the work itself
and the relationship between workers and the entities that direct them.
Extremely productive in computing: processes are workers, schedulers are
supervisors, tasks are submitted and completed, and failing processes
are killed or terminated.
