The open-air market or bazaar: a decentralized space where many independent
vendors sell goods to self-directed buyers. No central authority controls
what is sold, quality emerges from reputation and competition, and the
ambient noise of overlapping transactions is a feature, not a bug. The
frame foregrounds choice, diversity, low barriers to entry, and the
productive chaos of many agents pursuing their own interests. Structurally
useful for mapping any system where coordination happens through
self-interest rather than hierarchy.
