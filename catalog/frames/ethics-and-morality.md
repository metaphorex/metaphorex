The domain of right and wrong, good and evil, duty and transgression. Covers
moral reasoning, ethical judgment, conscience, guilt, innocence, and the social
systems that enforce moral norms. As a target domain it draws heavily on
physical source frames -- accounting (moral debts and credits), cleanliness
(moral purity), straightness (moral rectitude), vertical orientation (moral
standing) -- because moral experience is abstract and needs concrete structure
to become thinkable and communicable. Lakoff's *Moral Politics* (1996) argues
that entire political worldviews are organized by different metaphorical
mappings onto this domain.
