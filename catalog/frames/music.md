The production and perception of organized sound. As a source domain for
metaphor, music supplies models of harmony (parts fitting together),
rhythm (recurring temporal patterns), resonance (sympathetic vibration),
and tuning (precise calibration). Its metaphorical power comes from the
fact that music is both rigorously structural -- governed by mathematical
ratios of frequency -- and deeply felt as embodied experience. When we say
people are "in harmony" or "in tune," we borrow not just an aesthetic
image but an implicit physics of compatible vibration.
