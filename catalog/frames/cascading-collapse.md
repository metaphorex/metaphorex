A mode of systemic failure where multiple independent stressors interact
and compound over time, producing an outcome worse than any individual
crisis would cause alone. Distinguished from sudden catastrophe by its
temporal distribution (decades, not days) and from gradual decline by
its nonlinear interactions (each crisis amplifies the others). The frame
imports assumptions about invisibility (the collapse is hard to perceive
while living through it), survivorship bias (those who emerge are not
representative), and retrospective legibility (the pattern is only
visible after the fact).
