The behavior of electrical energy -- current flowing through conductors,
voltage driving charge, circuits completing or breaking, resistance
impeding flow, short-circuits bypassing intended paths. As a source
domain, electricity supplies metaphors of invisible energy entering and
animating a body, sudden jolts, scalar intensity (from mild buzz to
destructive overload), and binary on/off states. The domain became
culturally available in the 19th century with the spread of electrical
technology, and its metaphorical exports reflect the awe and danger
that early users associated with this invisible, powerful force.
