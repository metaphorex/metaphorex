The inner life of the mind — cognition, emotion, sanity, and psychological
well-being. Where intellectual-inquiry focuses on the pursuit of knowledge,
mental-experience covers how we talk about the mind itself: its functioning
and malfunctioning, its fragility and resilience, its states of health and
collapse. As a target domain it draws on machines, brittle objects, containers,
and other physical source frames to give structure to subjective experience
that would otherwise resist description.
