The narrative structure of Mary Shelley's *Frankenstein; or, The Modern
Prometheus* (1818): a creator assembles something new from existing parts,
animates it, recoils from what they have made, and suffers the consequences
of abandonment rather than of creation itself. The frame is distinct from
dystopian fiction -- it predates the genre by over a century and operates
through Gothic horror and Romantic tragedy rather than political allegory.
Its roles -- creator, creation, hubris, abandonment, revolt, irreversibility
-- map onto technology risk scenarios where the central question is not
whether to create but whether to take responsibility for what is created.
