The regulation of who or what may perform operations on protected
resources. Access control systems define subjects (users, processes),
resources (files, memory, network endpoints), and the permissions that
connect them. The domain includes authentication (proving identity),
authorization (checking permissions), and auditing (recording access).
Computing borrowed its access control vocabulary almost entirely from
governance and institutional administration.
