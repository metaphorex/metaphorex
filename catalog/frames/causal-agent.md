The folk model of a willful entity that causes change: an agent acts on
a patient, producing an effect. The agent may heal, destroy, reveal, or
reshape — what matters is that causation is attributed to a purposeful
being rather than to impersonal process. Key logic: the agent chooses to
act, could have acted otherwise, and bears responsibility for the
outcome. Source domain for personification metaphors where abstract forces
(time, fate, nature) are cast as intentional actors.
