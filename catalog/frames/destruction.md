The breaking, smashing, and demolition of physical objects and structures.
As a source domain, destruction provides vivid imagery for any process that
reduces something functional to something ruined: hammering, smashing,
wrecking, demolishing, shattering. The frame emphasizes sudden violent force
applied to a thing that cannot withstand it, the moment of impact, and the
resulting wreckage. Unlike war, which involves strategy, sides, and
territorial stakes, destruction focuses narrowly on the act of breaking and
its aftermath.
