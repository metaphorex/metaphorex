The process by which a small initial provocation cascades into a
disproportionately large conflict. The frame highlights the gap between
cause and consequence, the accumulation of sunk costs and grievances that
lock participants into continued escalation, and the point at which the
original trigger becomes irrelevant to the conflict's own momentum.
Distinct from war itself: war is a state, escalation is the dynamic that
produces and sustains it.
