The structural organization of a biological organism: specialized organs
performing distinct functions, hierarchical control systems, circulatory
distribution of resources, immune defense against foreign agents, metabolic
processes of consumption and waste, and the arc from growth through maturity
to decay. Distinct from embodied-experience (which covers phenomenological
sensation from the inside) — organism logic views the body as an
engineering system from the outside, emphasizing functional
interdependence, systemic health, and the consequences of organ failure
for the whole.
