The study of how people behave within organizations -- hierarchies,
incentive structures, promotion ladders, bureaucratic dysfunction, and
the gap between formal authority and actual competence. As a source
domain for metaphor, organizational behavior supplies models of systemic
failure that feel intuitively right to anyone who has worked in a large
institution: people promoted beyond their abilities, committees that
optimize for process over outcome, and structures that reward the wrong
behaviors. These patterns map readily onto software systems, which
exhibit analogous forms of structural dysfunction.
