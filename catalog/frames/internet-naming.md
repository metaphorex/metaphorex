The system by which human-readable names are mapped to machine-readable
addresses on the internet. Internet naming is fundamentally a problem of
indirection -- translating memorable labels into routable numbers -- and
its vocabulary borrows heavily from territorial governance. Domains, zones,
delegation, and authority are all terms imported from political
administration to describe how naming rights are distributed across a
hierarchical system of registries and resolvers.
