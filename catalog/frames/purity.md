The domain of substance purity and contamination: the binary state of being
unmixed versus adulterated. Unlike cleanliness (which concerns surfaces,
washing, and hygiene processes), purity concerns the essential composition
of a substance — whether it is unadulterated, how much contaminant renders
it impure, and whether contamination is reversible. Key logic: a single
drop of contaminant can render an entire substance impure (asymmetric
contamination), purity is an absolute rather than a gradient, and testing
for purity requires assay rather than inspection. Source domain for moral,
religious, and ethnic purity discourses.
