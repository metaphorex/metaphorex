Bounded enclosures that hold, protect, and regulate access to their contents.
One of Lakoff and Johnson's primary image schemas — we understand categories,
emotions, and institutions as containers with insides, outsides, and
boundaries. The frame's simplicity makes it one of the most universally
mapped structures in human cognition.
