Computer-generated environments that simulate physical presence, ranging
from fully immersive headset experiences to persistent online worlds
accessed through screens. Virtual reality occupies an unusual position as
a target domain: it is itself a metaphor (the "reality" is virtual, not
actual), yet it is also a real technology with engineering constraints,
market dynamics, and social consequences. The domain is structured almost
entirely by spatial metaphors borrowed from physical space -- users
"enter" worlds, "walk" through environments, and "meet" other users --
even when the underlying technology is networked computation with no
inherent spatiality.
