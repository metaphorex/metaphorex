The domain of how parts are arranged into wholes: organizational charts,
system architectures, management hierarchies, taxonomies, and any abstract
structure where components occupy positions relative to one another. Key
logic: structures can be top-down or bottom-up, components can be
load-bearing or peripheral, layers can be added or removed, and the whole
can be coherent or fragmented. Distinct from architecture-and-building
(which emphasizes physical construction) — this frame covers the abstract
relational patterns that result from organizing.
