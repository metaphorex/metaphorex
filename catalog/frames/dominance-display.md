Ritualized behaviors where individuals establish social hierarchy without
(usually) resorting to actual combat. Found across species — from gorilla
chest-beating to peacock tail displays to boardroom posturing.

Key structure: two or more **challengers** perform **display behaviors** in
front of an **audience**. The display is costly (energy, risk, time) as a
signal of fitness. The **outcome** determines rank without requiring the
destruction of the loser — which is what makes it a *display* rather than
a *fight*.
