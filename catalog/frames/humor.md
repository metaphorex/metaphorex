The domain of humor, comedy, and laughter -- jokes, punchlines, wit, and
the involuntary physical response they provoke. Humor involves a setup and
a violation of expectation; the punchline lands or falls flat. The frame
includes both the social performance (comedian, audience, timing) and the
physiological response (laughter, tears, aching sides). As a target domain,
humor borrows heavily from physical violence (killing, slaying, cracking
up) and from combat (a battle of wits, a cutting remark).
