The process by which a dominant collective absorbs individuals or smaller
groups, overwriting their identity while retaining their useful capabilities.
The absorbed entity does not cease to exist -- it ceases to be itself. Key
structural elements include the asymmetry between collective and individual,
the promise or threat that resistance is meaningless, and the one-directional
nature of the transformation: once assimilated, the process cannot be reversed.
