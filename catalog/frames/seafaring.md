The domain of sailing ships and ocean navigation, one of the richest
source domains in the English language. Centuries of maritime commerce,
warfare, and exploration deposited hundreds of metaphors into everyday
speech -- most now completely detached from their nautical origins.
The frame is structurally productive because seafaring involves managing
unpredictable forces (wind, tide, current), navigating toward distant
goals, maintaining a complex system (the ship), and coordinating a
crew under hierarchy. These elements map readily onto business, politics,
relationships, and cognition.
