The domain of covering and uncovering: a surface extended over objects
spread below, with structural concerns of coverage, gaps, fit, stretch,
and exposure. Distinct from containers (which enclose contents within
boundaries) — covers operate on a surface-over-ground topology rather
than an inside/outside one. Key logic: a cover can be too thin (revealing
what's beneath), have gaps (leaving areas exposed), stretch to fit (at the
cost of thinness), or overlap (creating redundancy). Source domain for
metaphors about theories, explanations, and accounts that attempt to
encompass a set of facts.
