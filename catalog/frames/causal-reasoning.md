The everyday cognitive activity of understanding why things happen --
identifying causes, predicting effects, assigning responsibility, and
constructing explanations. As a target domain, causal reasoning is
deeply shaped by embodied metaphors of force, contact, and motion.
It spans informal attribution ("what made this happen?") through
scientific and legal standards of evidence, but the metaphorical
structure is most visible in ordinary language where causes push,
pull, produce, and block.
