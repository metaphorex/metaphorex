The folk model of insanity and mental derangement: a departure from a
rational baseline into a state of lost control, irrationality, delusion,
and obsession, with the possibility of recovery back to sanity. Distinct
from the clinical psychiatric model — the folk model treats madness as
binary (sane/insane), assumes a rational default state that madness
disrupts, and draws on historical frameworks of demonic possession and
moral failure. Source domain for metaphors about intense emotion,
irrational behavior, and loss of self-governance.
