The structured relationship between humans and the divine, organized around
worship, doctrine, and the attributes of gods. Monotheistic traditions
concentrate supreme attributes in a single deity — omniscience (knows all),
omnipotence (can do all), omnipresence (is everywhere) — and treat any rival
claim to those attributes as heresy. Productive as a source domain because
it maps the logical structure of divine authority (concentration of power,
universal dependence, unchallengeable centrality) onto systems where similar
structures appear as design problems rather than virtues.
