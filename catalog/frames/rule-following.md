The domain of agents orienting their behavior toward rules, norms, and
standards. The central relationship is between a rule-follower and the rule
itself: the follower must recognize the rule, interpret it, and maintain
conduct that satisfies it. Compliance is the maintained alignment between
behavior and norm; deviation is the departure. Unlike governance, which
concerns the administration and creation of rules, rule-following concerns
the follower's relationship to the rule as experienced from the inside --
the ongoing effort of staying in conformity and the ever-present possibility
of violation.
