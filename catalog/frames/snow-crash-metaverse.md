The shared virtual reality environment in Neal Stephenson's *Snow Crash*
(1992): a persistent, three-dimensional digital world accessed through
goggles and earphones, where users appear as customizable avatars and
navigate a single grand boulevard (the Street). The Metaverse in the
novel is explicitly a class-stratified space -- the rich have detailed
avatars and prime real estate, while the poor have grainy, low-resolution
representations and must use public terminals. As a source domain, the
Snow Crash Metaverse provides a complete spatial metaphor for online
social interaction, one in which the internet is not a network of pages
but a place you physically inhabit.
