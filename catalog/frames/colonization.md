The establishment of control over new territory by an external power,
involving settlement, resource extraction, and the imposition of the
colonizer's systems onto the colonized space. As a source domain,
colonization imports assumptions about rightful expansion, resource
entitlement, and the relationship between "civilization" and
"wilderness" that carry significant political freight. The frame is
productive precisely because it is contested: calling something
colonization is always also a moral claim about power and legitimacy.
