The patterns of interaction, influence, and organization that emerge when
people act in proximity: status hierarchies, norm enforcement, in-group
and out-group formation, collective behavior, and social contagion. As a
target domain, social dynamics receives frames from physics (social
"forces," "attraction," "friction"), biology (organisms, populations,
fitness), and engineering (systems, feedback loops). As a source domain,
social dynamics provides vocabulary for modeling organizational behavior,
cultural change, and network effects. The frame assumes that individual
behavior is shaped by social context and that group-level patterns are
not simply the sum of individual intentions.
