The practice of exerting supernatural influence through rituals, words, or
objects. Magic operates outside ordinary causation: the caster directs power
at a target through spells, potions, or enchantments that bypass physical
law. Key structural features include the asymmetry between caster and target,
the involuntary nature of the effect on the target, and the possibility of
counter-spells that reverse or neutralize the original working. Productive
as a source domain because it provides a rich vocabulary for describing
hidden causation, irresistible influence, and altered perception.
