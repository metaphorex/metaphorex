Being situated in a place, with the place's properties affecting the occupant.
A more static complement to the journeys frame -- where journeys emphasize
movement along a path, spatial location emphasizes positional stasis: you are
somewhere, and where you are matters. The frame provides depth (how far in),
boundaries (edges you can approach or cross), and regions (zones with distinct
properties). Grounded in the embodied experience of occupying space, this frame
underlies the STATES ARE LOCATIONS family of conceptual metaphors.
