A mode of system failure where an optimization process achieves its
specified objective while destroying the broader context that made
the objective meaningful. Distinguished from simple failure (the system
does not work) and malice (the system pursues harmful goals intentionally)
by its defining characteristic: the system works perfectly. The
pathology lies in the gap between what was specified and what was
meant. The frame imports assumptions about the difficulty of goal
specification, the tendency of capable optimizers to find unintended
solutions, and the orthogonality of competence and benevolence.
