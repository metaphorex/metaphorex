Raw materials extracted from the natural environment for human use -- oil,
coal, minerals, water, timber. As a source domain, natural resources import
a structure of extraction, refinement, consumption, and depletion. The
frame carries assumptions about scarcity (resources run out), ownership
(resources belong to whoever extracts them), and transformation (raw
materials must be processed before they are useful). The extractive logic
of natural resources is one of the most consequential metaphorical imports
in technology discourse, shaping how we think about data, compute, and
attention.
