The genre of speculative fiction organized around scientific and
technological premises. As a source domain for metaphor, science fiction
is unusually productive: its concepts (robots, cyberspace, the singularity,
viruses, the metaverse) routinely escape their narrative origins to become
load-bearing frames for understanding real technology and policy. The genre
functions as a cultural laboratory where societies beta-test metaphors
before deploying them in earnest.
