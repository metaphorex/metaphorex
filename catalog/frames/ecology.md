The study of relationships between organisms and their environments: how
species compete and cooperate, how energy flows through food webs, how
ecosystems maintain equilibrium or tip into collapse. As a source domain,
ecology provides vocabulary for complex interdependence, fragility under
stress, and emergent stability. Applied widely to organizational systems
("platform ecosystem," "startup ecosystem"), economic analysis (niches,
carrying capacity, competitive dynamics), and technology discourse (where
software stacks are modeled as living ecologies with keystone species and
extinction events). The frame imports: health means diversity, collapse
can be sudden and nonlinear, intervention can produce unexpected cascades.
