The study of organisms and their relationships to their surroundings —
how conditions shape behavior, how species adapt to habitats, and how
changes in the environment propagate through a system. Distinguished
from natural selection by its focus on the organism-environment
relationship rather than differential reproduction. A productive source
domain for software concepts where context silently shapes execution:
environment variables, runtime environments, and deployment ecosystems
all borrow the ecological vocabulary of habitats, conditions, and
adaptation.
