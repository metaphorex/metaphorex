The practice of diagnosing, treating, and curing illness in a living body.
As a source domain, medicine provides a rich causal structure: symptoms
indicate underlying conditions, diagnosis precedes treatment, treatment
aims at cure or management, and recovery is never guaranteed. The frame
is especially productive for abstract targets (relationships, economies,
societies) because it implies that problems have identifiable causes and
that expert intervention can restore health.
