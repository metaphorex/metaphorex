The domain of material substances and their intrinsic properties: hardness,
grain, texture, composition, temper, brittleness, malleability. Distinct from
manufacturing (which concerns production processes) and physical-objects
(which concerns discrete things). Materials logic foregrounds what something
is made of and how that composition determines behavior under stress —
whether it bends or breaks, holds an edge or dulls, resists corrosion or
decays. Source domain for metaphors about character, personality, and moral
constitution.
