The domain of dangers arising from human technological creation. Technology
risk centers on the gap between intention and outcome: the creator builds
something for one purpose, but the creation produces effects the creator
did not foresee, cannot reverse, and may not survive. The frame encompasses
both specific failure modes (runaway processes, cascading effects,
irreversibility) and the broader moral question of whether certain things
should be built at all. As a target domain, it draws heavily on mythological
and literary source frames -- Prometheus, Pandora, Frankenstein -- because
the abstract structure of technology risk (hubris, unforeseen consequences,
irreversibility) maps cleanly onto ancient narrative patterns.
