The high-level structure of software systems: how components are divided,
how they communicate, and where boundaries are drawn. Encompasses
monoliths and microservices, layers and hexagons, APIs and message buses.
Frequently a target domain for organizational and biological metaphors
because the structural decisions in software architecture -- coupling,
cohesion, interface design -- have direct analogues in how human groups
and living systems organize themselves.
