The production and interpretation of written text. Writing involves an author
composing inscriptions in a notation system, and a reader decoding them. Unlike
speech, writing fixes meaning in durable form -- creating a persistent artifact
that can be revised, copied, and consulted independently of its creator. The
frame foregrounds the gap between what is written and what is meant, the craft
of composition, and the requirement that readers possess the literacy to decode
what authors have inscribed.
