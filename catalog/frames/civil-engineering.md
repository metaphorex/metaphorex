The design, construction, and maintenance of infrastructure -- bridges, roads,
dams, tunnels, and the systems that connect human settlements. Civil engineering
vocabulary enters software through structural metaphors: bridges that span gaps,
load-bearing elements that support traffic, foundations that enable everything
above them. The domain is rich because it foregrounds the tension between two
sides that need connecting, the cost of spanning distance, and the difference
between static structure and dynamic load. Unlike architecture proper, civil
engineering emphasizes public infrastructure over individual buildings,
making it a natural source for metaphors about systems that serve many users
under variable conditions.
