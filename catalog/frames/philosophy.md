The systematic examination of fundamental questions: the nature of reality
(metaphysics), knowledge and justification (epistemology), right action
(ethics), consciousness and mind (philosophy of mind), and the structure
of valid reasoning (logic). As a source domain, philosophy contributes
the tools of rigorous conceptual analysis -- thought experiments, reductios
ad absurdum, definitional precision -- to other fields. In common usage,
invoking the philosophy frame elevates a practical question to a fundamental
one: "that's a philosophical question" signals that the answer is not
obvious and that the conceptual terrain needs mapping before progress is
possible. The frame imports: precision in definition matters, edge cases
reveal the truth of general principles, and abstraction is a tool not
an evasion.
