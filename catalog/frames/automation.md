The use of machines to perform tasks previously done by humans, from
assembly lines to software agents. Automation sits at the intersection of
tool-use and labor: the machine is both an instrument (controlled by
someone) and a worker (replacing someone). This dual identity makes
automation a persistent site of metaphorical tension -- is the robot a
tool or a colleague? A servant or a replacement? The frame's internal
ambiguity about agency is what makes it so productive as a target domain.
