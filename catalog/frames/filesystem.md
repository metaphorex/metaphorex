The organizational structure of persistent storage in an operating system --
a hierarchy of named containers (directories) holding named data objects
(files), rooted at a single origin point and navigated by path expressions.
The filesystem frame is one of the most heavily metaphorized domains in
computing: its root comes from botany, its mounting from physical equipment,
its tree structure from horticulture, and its navigation from geography. As
a target frame, it is the thing being *understood through* these source
domains rather than a metaphor itself. The frame's key structural features
are hierarchical namespace resolution, path-based addressing, and the
distinction between containers (directories) and content (files).
