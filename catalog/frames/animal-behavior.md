The repertoire of wild animal conduct: hunting, fleeing, mating, territorial
marking, dominance displays, pack hierarchy, and seasonal migration. Distinct
from animal husbandry in that no human manager is present — the animals act
on instinct, drive, and evolutionary programming rather than under
domestication. Source domain for metaphors about human regression, loss of
rational control, raw desire, and social hierarchy. When people say someone
"is an animal," they reach for this frame — predation, appetite, mating
frenzy — not the managed world of herds and pastures.
