Risks that threaten the continued existence or fundamental character of
human civilization. As a target domain, existential risk is structured
by competing source metaphors: extinction events (asteroid impact),
slow decline (civilizational decay), arms races (nuclear proliferation),
and alignment failures (AI risk). The frame carries assumptions about
scale (global, not local), irreversibility (no recovery if the risk
materializes), and the difficulty of probabilistic reasoning about
events with no historical precedent.
