Structured transmission of knowledge from those who have it to those who
do not. The frame encompasses pedagogy (how to teach), curriculum (what to
teach), assessment (whether it was learned), and the social hierarchy of
teacher and student. As a source domain, education imports strong
assumptions about intentionality (the teacher designs the learning
experience), comprehension (the student understands, not merely memorizes),
and progression (learning advances through stages toward mastery). These
assumptions make it a powerful but misleading frame when applied to
processes that lack intentionality or understanding.
