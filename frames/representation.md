The act of standing in for something else — models, symbols, proxies, and
surrogates. Every representation involves a trade-off between fidelity and
utility, and every act of modeling is also an act of omission. Structurally
central to metaphor theory itself, since metaphors are a form of
representation where one domain stands in for another.
